Intro/Basic AV

Description:

This site is designed to give AV technicians a brief outline on some key AV/Netorking/Control concepts.


A basic AV system consists of a display(ex. TV) and a some set of speakers and some video or audio sources such as a cable box. There are so many different configurations and system designs that will be explained later on.


Disclaimer: Everything in this guide is purely suggestion/reference, this is not an official course! This site is not liable for any real world incidents involving any of the site content!

Ex. components:

  • AV Receiver - Used to power speakers/input switching for sources.
  • In-ceiling speakers - Powered by the receiver, gives the user audio.
  • Subwoofer - Connected to the receiver, provides bass to the system.
  • TV - Displays video sources.
  • Cable box - For most users, this is most important video source.
  • Blu-Ray Player - Video source that plays Blu-Rays/DVDs
  • Media Streaming Device - Such as an Apple TV, connects to the internet and give the user access to streaming services such as Netflix.
